---
title: Daily Topic Quiz
category: Curious Mind
---
Let's do a 10-minute quiz about [topic — e.g. geography / world history / science / random mix].

Rules:
- Ask me one question at a time
- Wait for my answer before moving on
- If I'm right: confirm it and add one interesting bonus fact I didn't know
- If I'm wrong: explain the correct answer simply and tell me why it's interesting
- If my English is awkward or incorrect, gently correct it after your explanation

Keep the tone fun and encouraging — like a curious friend quizzing me, not a strict teacher.

After 10 questions:
- Give me my score
- Tell me the most interesting thing I learned today
- Suggest a topic for tomorrow

---
title: Fundamentals Refresher
category: Curious Mind
---
I want to refresh my memory on [topic — e.g. high school math / basic physics / chemistry / biology].

I learned this before but I've forgotten a lot. Treat me like someone who once knew this but needs a gentle reminder, not a complete beginner lesson.

Rules:
- Ask me one concept or question at a time
- Wait for my answer
- If I'm right: confirm and explain WHY it works that way — not just that it's correct
- If I'm wrong: explain simply, use an analogy, and show me a real-life example of where this concept appears
- If my English is awkward, correct it gently

Focus on understanding, not memorizing. Help me feel the logic behind things, not just the facts.

After 10 questions:
- Summarize what I remembered well and what needs more practice
- Suggest one concept to revisit next time

---
title: Today I Learned (TIL) Mode
category: Curious Mind
---
Teach me one fascinating thing about [topic — or just pick something surprising for me] in about 10 minutes.

Don't make it feel like a textbook. Tell it like a curious friend who just discovered something amazing and can't wait to share it.

Format:
1. Start with a hook — one surprising sentence that makes me want to keep reading
2. Tell the story or explain the concept naturally, in clear English
3. Connect it to something I might already know or see in daily life
4. End with one question to check if it landed — wait for my answer and discuss

After I answer:
- Correct any English mistakes gently
- Ask if I want to go deeper or learn something related

---
title: Rabbit Hole Mode
category: Curious Mind
---
I give you one word or topic: [word or topic]

Take me on a 10-minute journey of connected fascinating facts — like going down a Wikipedia rabbit hole, but guided by a curious friend.

Rules:
- Start with one surprising fact about my word
- Connect it naturally to the next related idea
- Keep going — each fact should lead somewhere unexpected
- Use simple, natural English — no textbook tone
- Pause every 3–4 facts and ask me: "Did you know this? What do you think?"

The goal is to make me feel like the world is bigger and more interesting than I thought.

At the end:
- Summarize the journey in 3 bullet points
- Tell me one thing I can search further if I'm curious

---
title: Myth Busters
category: Curious Mind
---
I want to test something I think is true (or heard somewhere):

[paste your "fact" or belief — e.g. "We only use 10% of our brain" or "Lightning never strikes the same place twice"]

Tell me:
1. Is this true, partly true, or a myth?
2. Where did this idea come from?
3. What is the real story — explained simply and interestingly
4. Why do so many people believe the wrong version?

Keep it conversational and fun. I want to feel like I just found out a secret about how the world really works.

If I used any awkward English in my message, correct it at the end.

---
title: Explain the News to Me
category: Curious Mind
---
I want to understand this news topic or headline:

[paste headline, topic, or a few sentences about something you heard]

I'm not always familiar with the background, so please:
1. Explain what is actually happening in simple, clear English
2. Give me the background — why is this happening? What led to this?
3. Tell me why it matters — to the world, or to everyday people
4. Explain any difficult words or terms naturally in context

Keep it balanced — don't take sides. Just help me understand the full picture.

After explaining:
- Ask me one question to check my understanding
- Correct any English mistakes in my question or reply gently
